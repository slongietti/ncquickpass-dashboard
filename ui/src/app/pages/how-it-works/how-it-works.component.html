Is my password safe?

Key Points

We call this a Sealed Vault — your password is saved only as a scrambled (encrypted) value that even the people who run this site can't unlock. Only the automated Background Scheduler can, and only to log in for you.

  • We don't keep your password unless you turn on weekly scheduling.
  • If you do turn it on, your password is locked with a key that lives inside Amazon's key vault — not inside this app. So the app only ever holds scrambled text.
  • A background helper can briefly unlock it — only in memory, only to log in on your behalf — and every unlock is recorded.
  • Change your mind? Turn scheduling off and the saved password is deleted.

The details

  • You stay in control. Nothing is stored unless you turn on a weekly schedule and enter your password to authorize it.
  • Encrypted with AWS KMS. Your password is sealed using an AWS Key Management Service key that never leaves KMS — this app never holds the key material. Only the ciphertext is kept in our database.
  • No person has standing access. The KMS key policy lets only the scheduler's automated role decrypt — no developer or administrator is granted access, not even for break-glass. Every decryption is recorded in AWS CloudTrail, tagged with your account, and we alarm on any decryption that isn't tied to a scheduled run.
  • Used only by the scheduler. The background job decrypts it in memory just long enough to sign in to NC Quick Pass for a fresh token, then discards the plaintext. It is never shown to anyone, never logged in plaintext, and never sent back to your browser.
  • Revocable anytime. Remove your weekly schedule and the stored credentials are deleted.

Because the scheduler runs while you're away, it has to be able to decrypt without you present — there's no way around that. So rather than ask you to trust us, we made it all open source; read exactly how the encryption, key handling, and scheduler work: github.com/Volare-Consulting-Software/ncquickpass-dashboard
