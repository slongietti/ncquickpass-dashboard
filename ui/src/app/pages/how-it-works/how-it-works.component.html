How scheduling protects your credentials

By default this app never stores your credentials — only a short-lived token lives in your browser. Automatic weekly scheduling is the one exception, and only if you opt in.

What happens to your password

  • You stay in control. Nothing is stored unless you turn on a weekly schedule and enter your password to authorize it.
  • Encrypted with AWS KMS. Your password is sealed using an AWS Key Management Service key that never leaves KMS — this app never holds the key material. Only the ciphertext is kept in our database.
  • No person has standing access. The KMS key policy lets only the scheduler's automated role decrypt — no developer or administrator is granted access, not even for break-glass. Every decryption is recorded in AWS CloudTrail, tagged with your account, and we alarm on any decryption that isn't tied to a scheduled run.
  • Used only by the scheduler. The background job decrypts it in memory just long enough to sign in to NC Quick Pass for a fresh token, then discards the plaintext. It is never shown to anyone, never logged in plaintext, and never sent back to your browser.
  • Revocable anytime. Remove your weekly schedule and the stored credentials are deleted.

You don't have to take our word for any of this. This entire application is open source — the encryption, the key handling, and the scheduler are all in public code you can read and audit (linked below). Because the scheduler has to run while you're away, the system must be able to decrypt without you present; there is no way around that. That's exactly why the implementation is public and verifiable rather than something you're asked to simply trust.
